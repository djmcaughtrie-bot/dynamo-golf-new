Technique Library 07

How to Play Different Shots

1W

Drive

Key: Wide stance + full shoulder turn

Use your driver (1-wood) for maximum distance off the tee. Tee the ball high, widen your stance, and sweep through the ball with a full shoulder turn. Focus on a smooth tempo rather than swinging hard.

3W

Fairway Wood

Used for long shots from the fairway. Play the ball slightly forward in your stance and brush the turf through impact. Great for par-5 second shots.

Key: Brush the turf, don't dig
5I

Iron Shot

Irons are used for approach shots to the green. Ball position moves progressively back toward centre as irons get shorter. Strike down and through the ball, taking a divot after impact.

Key: Descending strike, divot after ball
CHP

Chip

A low-running shot played from just off the green. Use a 7–9 iron or wedge with a narrow stance, hands ahead of the ball, and a short putting-style stroke. Aim to land the ball just on the green and let it run to the hole.

Key: Hands forward, minimal wrist
PW

Pitch

A higher, shorter shot into the green from 20–100 yards. Open your stance slightly, hinge the wrists on the backswing, and accelerate through impact. Use a pitching or sand wedge.

Key: Hinge wrists, accelerate through
SW

Bunker Shot

From a greenside bunker, open the clubface and your stance, aim 2 inches behind the ball, and splash the sand out with the ball. Don't try to hit the ball directly — let the sand do the work.

Key: Hit sand 2" behind, open face
PUT

Putt

Use your putter on the green. Keep your eyes over the ball, use a pendulum stroke with no wrist break, and focus on consistent tempo. Read the slope of the green to judge the line.

Key: Eyes over ball, pendulum stroke, read the green
